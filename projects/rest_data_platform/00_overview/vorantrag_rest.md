ReST Data Platform: Driving Regional Innovation Through Advanced Digital Technology 
Hanfeld, Kotzur, Schleuter, Schmidt
1. Introduction/Preliminary work (for the "first 15 pages")
1.1 Introduction
The ASTRA project (Applied Sustainable Transformation by Regional Anchors) is a pioneering initiative designed to promote sustainable development in East Frisia, addressing the region's growing economic, social, and environmental challenges. With a comprehensive approach across five specific work packages (WPs), ASTRA aims to embed sustainable practices deeply within the regional framework.
The ReST (Regional Sustainability and Transformation) Data Platform plays a pivotal role in this endeavor, serving as a link between the WPs and providing a robust data infrastructure. It functions as a central data hub and a platform for data collection, analysis, and visualization, supporting both the WPs and regional stakeholders. By offering relevant data and enhancing analytical capabilities, the ReST Data Platform becomes an essential tool for data-driven decision-making and the implementation of sustainable development strategies.
As a catalyst for innovation, the platform facilitates the efficient use of existing data sources and the integration of new information, enabling in-depth analysis of regional dynamics and the identification of future development opportunities. Thus, the ReST Data Platform significantly contributes to the long-term economic, social, and ecological sustainability of East Frisia, strengthening regional resilience against global challenges.
1.2 Preliminary work for ASTRA
In recent years, the project „Wissenschaftliche Begleitung des wirtschaftlichen Strukturwandels in Ostfriesland” has established an extensive database. This initiative aimed to support regional development and transformation through precise analyses and strategic recommendations. In this context, approximately 380 indicators were recorded, with 150 specifically prepared and visualized for the city of Emden and the districts of East Frisia. These data encompass a broad spectrum of topics including education, labor market, innovation, economy & prosperity, society/population, and infrastructure & mobility.
A key aspect of this work included detailed inventories and analyses of the current regional situation. Economic and social indicators were captured, best-practice examples identified, and the region's future viability assessed. The insights derived serve as the basis for a SWOT analysis, from which specific action needs are identified.
Successful Communication and Engagement Measures:
Innovation Culture Survey: From December 17, 2020, to February 5, 2021, 770 companies were contacted, with 102 responding. This survey provided cross-industry insights into the innovation culture of companies in East Frisia, helping to identify specific needs and challenges. 
Generation Z Survey – Future Prospects & Quality of Life: Between December 2021 and March 2022, 611 students with an average age of 18 were surveyed. The results offered valuable regional insights into the future prospects and quality of life of young people.
(Inter)Regional Exchange and Networking Meetings: Cooperation with numerous municipalities and institutions such as the Chamber of Commerce and Industry of East Frisia & Papenburg, NBank, and KPMG, as well as other regional actors, promoted knowledge exchange. Active participation in fairs and forums such as the Production & IT Forum in Leer, the Promotiedagen in Groningen, and the Research Forum at the University of Applied Sciences Emden/Leer enhanced regional networking.
Relevant Projects, Institutes & Networks:
•	Initiative Operational Excellence Nordwest: This initiative enhances regional networking and cross-industry exchange on best practices and operational excellence. It aims to assist businesses in the region to continuously improve their processes and become more efficient and sustainable.
•	Energiesystemmodellierung: Develops decarbonization scenarios and analyzes complex system interdependencies, with findings presented at international conferences to inform sustainable energy strategies for East Frisia.
•	Elektromobilität im ländlichen Raum: Undertaken by the Sustainable Mobility Transfer Center (TraNaMo) at the University of Applied Sciences Emden/Leer, this project aligns fluctuating wind energy supply with growing electric vehicle demands in rural areas, aiming to formulate sustainable mobility strategies and assess infrastructure expansion needs.
•	HILOG (Hochschulinstitut für Logistik): Focused on research and development in logistics and supply chain management, HILOG works closely with regional businesses to develop practical solutions to logistical challenges, enhance supply chain efficiency, and reduce costs.
•	greentech OSTFRIESLAND: Supports the development and implementation of sustainable technologies in East Frisia, aiding startups and established firms in reducing emissions and improving energy efficiency. The initiative facilitates knowledge exchange and collaboration through workshops, funding programs, and networking events.
The preliminary work and projects have established a solid foundation for the development and implementation of the ReST Data Platform within the ASTRA project. The data and insights gathered are of great value for ongoing analysis and strategic planning, which are crucial for the sustainable transformation of the region.
2. Description, Objectives, and Strategic Orientation of the ReST Data Platform for Regional Monitoring (for the Implementation Part)
The ReST Data Platform significantly contributes to the sustainable transformation and development of the Ostfriesland region by providing comprehensive data infrastructure and support for data-driven decisions. The platform promotes the achievement of the Sustainable Development Goals, particularly those for sustainable cities and communities (SDG 11) and partnerships for the goals (SDG 17), by offering evidence-based decision-making tools for sustainable development measures in the region.
2.1 Specific Objectives
•	Data Integration and Provision: The platform creates an accessible data base that integrates and provides economic, social, ecological, and demographic information as well as WP-specific data. This includes the aggregation, processing, and sharing of data arising from the activities of individual WPs, serving as a foundation for data-driven analysis and decision-making.
•	Promotion of Data-Driven Decisions: Support for the WPs of the ASTRA project and local decision-makers through sound data analysis and visualizations to develop sustainable strategies.
•	Improvement of Collaboration: Enhancement of knowledge exchange and cooperation between WPs and regional actors such as businesses, public institutions, and civil society.
•	Transparency and Public Relations: Increasing transparency about regional development processes and promoting understanding of sustainable developments among the public.
2.2 Long-Term Vision
The ReST Data Platform aims to become an essential part of the development strategy in East Frisa and serve as a central hub for data, analysis needs and knowledge acquisition. It strives to establish the region as a model for sustainable development and data-driven decision-making. By consistently working towards the achievement of the Sustainable Development Goals, it strengthens regional resilience and promotes sustainable progress.
2.3 Project Structure and Methodological Approach
Overview of the Project Structure
The ReST Data Platform supports the WPs with comprehensive data management and analysis capabilities. The Client Layer (Frontend) offers user-friendly interfaces, supported by a modern web framework. The Application Logic Layer (Backend) coordinates the central application logic and data communication. The Data Layer (Backend) utilizes PostgreSQL for data management, complemented by a REST API and metadata management. The Infrastructure Layer ensures flexibility and scalability with options like Private Cloud and virtual machines.
Methods and Procedures
The development and implementation of the ReST Data Platform occur step-by-step and include a needs analysis to capture the requirements of the WPs and stakeholders, continuous data integration to update the data base, technical development of backend and frontend components for a robust and user-friendly platform, advanced data analysis to generate actionable insights, creation of intuitive visual tools for data exploration, a pilot phase to optimize functionality and usability, and a full rollout with comprehensive training for all users.
Data Sources and Management
The platform draws information from public databases, specific surveys and studies, sentiment and company data. A comprehensive data management system ensures the secure storage, management, and accessibility of data.
2.4 Implementation of the ReST Data Platform
The implementation of the ReST Data Platform is executed in several phases. Initially, the existing data base is integrated and progressively expanded with new data sources. This includes data aggregation, cleansing, and standardization. The platform employs advanced technologies for data analysis and processing, including descriptive, predictive, and prescriptive analytics. For data visualization, user-friendly tools such as interactive dashboards and Geographic Information Systems (GIS) are provided, simplifying complex data interpretation. A Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system leverages large language models (LLMs) to enable natural data interaction and deliver precise, contextualized responses, significantly enhancing decision-making quality and supporting strategic planning.
2.5 Usage and Benefits of the ReST Data Platform
The ReST Data Platform offers extensive functionalities both internally and externally, crucial for enhancing regional development and sustainability. Internally, the platform aids the ASTRA project's WPs with a comprehensive data base and visualization tools that facilitate efficient data management, profound analysis capabilities, and intuitive data interaction. Externally, it serves as a valuable resource for external stakeholders by providing comprehensive information and analyses, increasing transparency, and offering enhanced decision support through the RAG system. The platform acts as a catalyst for new initiatives, promotes regional sustainability, drives economic growth, and improves quality of life through targeted measures. 
2.6 Relevant WPs, Projects, and Collaborations
Within the ASTRA project, which aims at sustainable transformation, the ReST Data Platform plays a central role in supporting and connecting the work packages (WPs).
WP 1: Sustainable transformation of corporates - The ReST Data Platform facilitates the transformation of business processes by providing data analysis and insights essential for digitalizing social services, analyzing the sustainability of industrial processes, optimizing energy efficiency through "Energycloud" technologies, and detailed analysis and evaluation of ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) performance of companies. This includes developing improved sustainability strategies, supported by the RAG system for efficient data processing and comparative analysis. 
WP 2: Circular economy in regional context - The platform enables the integration and analysis of data to develop a circular economy in the maritime sector and to scale hydrogen technologies in maritime applications. It supports the design of carbon-neutral wastewater treatment plants and sustainable economic systems.
WP 3: Well-being in rural areas - The ReST Data Platform supports WP3 by providing data on public health and culture, as well as the impacts of climate change on health. It enables the development of informed health strategies by providing epidemiological data for monitoring and evaluating pilot interventions. Additionally, the platform supports the creation of actionable recommendations, thus promoting further research approaches and the scalability of health-promoting measures. 
WP 4: ASTRA Living labs - Transfer centers act as interfaces between research and application. The ReST Data Platform provides easily accessible and applicable scientific knowledge and technological innovations to effectively support knowledge transfer and facilitate collaboration between universities, research institutions, and industry.
WP 5: Digital Service Technologies - In the area of Digital Service Technologies, the ReST Data Platform serves as the technological backbone of the entire ASTRA project. It supports the other work packages through comprehensive data management, analysis, and visualization, facilitating the development and implementation of innovative solutions and strategies.
Collaborations - The ASTRA project fosters diverse collaborations with key actors such as educational institutions, local and regional authorities, chambers of commerce, NGOs, and the private sector. The platform facilitates these partnerships by providing easy access to relevant data and supporting exchange and collaboration. By strengthening these comprehensive partnerships, the platform directly contributes to the implementation of SDG 17, which calls for enhancing the means of implementation and revitalizing the global partnership for sustainable development.
